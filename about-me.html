The Four Pillars Of Your Technology Group

The mission statement above is realized and brought to life when your technology group delivers excellence in these four primary areas: Performance, Reliability, Scalability and Cost. I'll briefly talk about each one of these and give examples of some key metrics. These are defined differently for every system, so my examples given below are generalized for web services.

Performance

The capabilities of your systems observed under particular conditions. Identify the key operating characteristics and a set of conditions under which to observe your systems. The better your systems perform, the better your company performs.

Key Metrics:

  • Time to First Byte
  • Page Load Time
  • CPU Processing Time (View execution time in a MVC world)
  • Bytes Downloaded
  • Total Number of Requests Required (especially important for service composition, which gets into how the services are defined and compartmentalized and how they service your users' requests.)

Success:

  • Bake performance measurement into your full software development life cycle (SDLC).
  • Publish the performance of key web pages or web services to the rest of your organization.
  • Constantly refactor implementations to accommodate changing user interactions and requirements.

Reliability

The consistency of all measures of your systems. How consistently your systems perform according to the characteristics and conditions identified above. A high degree of reliability in your systems is a primary driver of trust between you and your customers.

Key Metrics:

  • Low variance of performance measurements taken over time.

Success:

  • Reliability is an extension of performance
  • Make reliability testing is a core function of your QA group
  • Monitor error logs for uses that you didn't take into account

Scalability

The ability of your systems to continue operating with a similar degree of performance and reliability as their workload volume increases. Most commonly this is surfaced as a growing number of concurrent consumers of your web services.

Key Metrics:

  • Number of users or consumers
  • Number of requests per second served

Success:

  • Handle normal workloads
  • Handle surge workloads
  • Fail gracefully
  • Have monitoring in place so you know when you need to take action

Cost

Cost permeates every aspect of your organization from salaries, to time to market, to the actual cost of each request or customer serviced. The better we can measure cost the better we will be at optimizing systems and driving cost down.

Key Metrics:

  • People time
  • Machine time
  • Infrastructure
  • Third party services

Success:

  • Optimize for cost while maintaining the integrity of the other three pillars

Powering Your Organization Through Technology Leadership

There are two distinct components of the team. The people. And the technology that those people create. Together they are the lifeblood of any technology based organization. They will drag you behind your competition or spring-board you past your competition and set you up as the recognized leader and innovator in your space.

Technology Decision Maker

I believe every tech company needs a brilliant technology decision maker at the core of the team. This person is responsible for making the hard tech decisions. They are also responsible for driving the creation of something new, better, or different; something that sets your company apart from the pac. This person sets up the technology engine that drives the realization of your company's vision.

Technology People Leader

This person puts together a world class technology team and is the glue that keeps them together. This person understands what the team is doing and drives success by aligning the team with the broader company vision. This isn't so much decision making as it is decision guidance. This person expands the realm of what's possible in the minds of the company visionaries. In that capacity, this person helps focus the company and define and redefine the vision as the company grows. This person also acts as the tie breaker for tough decisions and weighs appropriately in what the company needs at that particular time.

I'm Somewhere In Between

I'm a people person, in that I care deeply about the people on my team. You won't hear me refer to them as resources; I will cringe when I hear that word applied to people. I will work to understand what motivates each individual of the team. I also have strong technology opinions and will put together the optimum technology stack for the time. I believe there is no right way to write code; I believe the most important aspect of the implementation at any given time is our ability to evolve it. The best architecture is is implemented for now, with an understanding what it will become tomorrow.
